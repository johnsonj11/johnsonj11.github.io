9 Chapters 9 and 10 problems

  1. An experiment is planned to test a new treatment to help people to quit smoking. Volunteer smokers (who wish to quit) will be randomly allocated to one of two groups. In group 1, the volunteers will use the new treatment for two months. They will be recorded as “quitting” if they do not smoke at all for a six month period following treatment. The second group will act as a control group, in which the volunteers will instead be given a placebo, but then monitored in the same way. A total of \(2n\) volunteers will be recruited, with \(n\) volunteers allocated to each group.
  • The data (the proportions quitting in each group) will be analysed with a Neyman-Pearson hypothesis test, with size 0.05.
  • The investigators want to detect a difference of at least 10% in the proportions quitting in the two groups. They suggest assuming that 55% will quit in the treatment group, and 45% will quit in the control group.
  • The investigators want their study to have a power of 80%.

What sample size \(n\) per group would you recommend? Some R output to help you is below.

qnorm(c(0.8, 0.975))
## [1] 0.8416 1.9600
